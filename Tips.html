In General

  1. Pay attention when the lesson is being taught.
  2. Ask questions when something isn't understood.
  3. Study a little everyday. Don't just cram the night before.

Math

  1. Learn the formulas in an easy to remember way like mnemonic devices.
  2. Make sure you understand everything from the beginning since it's cumulative.
  3. Have the proper tools such as a ruler and protractor among others.

Language Arts

  1. When writing essays finish it a day before the deadline and get it checked over by the teacher.
  2. To write a speech start talking and write it down and revise after you finsih writing.
  3. If you don't understand a book that is assigned use a website like sparknotes to get a better picture of the plot.

Science

  1. Make flash cards of science vocabulary.
  2. Science innvolves lots of math so study the formulas.
  3. Pay attention to the experiments and labs because most likely there will questions related to that on the test.

Social Studies

  1. Take notes of dates and locations of historical events using different colors.
  2. It involves a lot of memorization so take good notes.
  3. It's really hard to memorize an entire era of history in one night so study notes daily.

Foreign Language

  1. Write the vocab while reciting it to solidify it in your mind.
  2. Make flashcards using something like Quizlet.
  3. Write the pronunciation next to the words.

Art

  1. Use class time wisely.
  2. Pay attention to the demonstrations of how to do the art project.
  3. Learn the art terms because there will most likely be a test.
